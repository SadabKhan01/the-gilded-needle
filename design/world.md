# The Gilded Needle — World & Narrative Design
*Narrative & World Designer doc. Builds on the canonical skeleton: tiers poor/middle/noble/royal; zones old_mill, river_warehouse, silk_grove, palace_vaults; maps city, shop, palace_hall; 30–45 min playthrough.*

---

## 1. NAMES

**Heroine candidates:** Marielle Thimm · Odette Vance · Greta Fenwick
**PICK: Marielle Thimm** ("Thimm" echoes *thimble*; friends call her "Mari").

**City candidates:** Auberlin · Bellcourt · Gildehaven
**PICK: Auberlin** — the grand river-and-rail capital, "the city where every street wears a different coat."

**Home village:** **Hartsfell** — a wool-farming village high in the Kammerite Mountains, snow eight months a year.

**Late mentor:** **Grandmother Brida Thimm**, called "the Needle of Hartsfell." She once sewed for Auberlin's old queen before retiring to the mountains in quiet disgrace after a rival tailor stole credit for her masterwork. She raised Marielle, taught her every stitch, and left her two things: a one-way train ticket to Auberlin, and a brass needle plated in true gold that never bends — *the gilded needle*. Marielle's journey is quietly also Brida's unfinished one.

## 2. OPENING CINEMATIC (skippable; parallax backdrops, no walking)

1. **Black screen, a single gold stitch draws itself across the dark.**
   Narration: *"Grandmother always said a life is sewn one stitch at a time. She never said how far the thread might run."*

2. **Hartsfell at dawn** — snowy rooftops, smoke from chimneys, Marielle at a garden gate with a carpet bag. Villagers wave; an old dog trots after her.
   Narration: *"Hartsfell gave me wool, weather, and everyone I had ever known. Grandmother Brida gave me the rest."*

3. **Close-up: her hands open a small tin.** Inside: the gilded needle on faded blue felt, and a train ticket.
   Narration: *"She left me a ticket she never used, and a needle that never bends. I intended to be worthy of both."*

4. **The mountain station.** A green-and-brass steam engine huffs in; the platform is one plank longer than the train.
   Narration: *"The 7:12 to Auberlin only stops in Hartsfell if you flag it down. I flagged."*

5. **Train ride, parallax:** near fence-posts blur past; mid-ground pine slopes give way to terraced fields; far mountains slide slowly, pink with morning. Marielle's reflection in the window over the moving world.
   Narration: *"The mountains let go of me slowly, the way Grandmother folded cloth — never in a hurry, never by accident."*

6. **Interior beat:** the Conductor punches her ticket, tips his cap. A sleeping merchant snores under a bowler hat. Marielle sketches a dress in a little notebook.
   Narration: *"Sixteen hours, one apple, and eleven dresses I drew for people I hadn't met yet."*

7. **Night window:** far off in the dark plain, a smudge of ten thousand lights.
   Narration: *"And then — Auberlin. A city stitched entirely out of lamplight."*

8. **Auberlin Grand Station:** iron arches, steam, pigeons, porters, a flower cart. Marielle small in the crowd, clutching her bag, eyes enormous.
   Narration: *"They say every street here wears a different coat. Well. Somebody has to mend them."*

9. **Fade to the plaza; a dusty little shopfront with a crooked sign: THE GILDED NEEDLE.** Player control begins.
   Narration: *"Stitch one."*

## 3. THE CITY (hub map)

One walkable hub: **Spindle Square**, a cobbled plaza with a copper fountain shaped as a spool of thread, radiating four district streets. **Marielle's shop, The Gilded Needle, faces the fountain** — narrow, two windows, crooked hand-painted sign, door bell that goes *ting*.

**Cinder Row (poor quarter, west):** Leaning timber houses, laundry lines webbing the street, patched everything. Palette: warm umbers and soot — #6B5645, #8C7358, #3E3630, with washing-line whites #E8E0D0. Sounds: flapping linen, a squeaky mangle, children playing skip-thread, distant foundry clank. Landmarks: **Berta's Washline**, a great communal wash-trough under a chestnut tree; **the Cold Chimney**, a defunct factory stack the kids dare each other to touch.

**Ribbon Row (market / middle-class, south):** Striped awnings, crate-stacked stalls, a haberdasher, a dye merchant, a pie window. Palette: market brights on warm brick — #C1443C awnings, #D9A441 mustard, #4E7B58 stall green, brick #9C5A3C. Sounds: haggling, a barrel organ, the *shk-shk* of scales, gulls up from the river. Landmarks: **the Dye Merchant's rainbow barrels** (Marsh & Daughter, Dyes); **the Ribbon Arch**, a wrought arch over the street where vendors tie a ribbon for every good market year.

**Larkspur Boulevard (noble, east):** Plane trees in iron cages, pale stone townhouses, gas lamps lit even at dusk-blue hour. Palette: cool creams and verdigris — #E4DCC8 stone, #7FA08C verdigris, #56617A slate roofs, gold trim #C9A54E. Sounds: carriage wheels on smooth stone, a distant string quartet from an open window, fountains. Landmarks: **the Larkspur Gates**, twin bronze herons guarding the Countess's townhouse garden; **Alba & Sons**, the rival tailor's gleaming three-window atelier (mannequins that always face you).

**Crownway (palace approach, north):** A broad processional rise of white flagstone, banner poles, guards in plum livery, the old palace dome floating above rooftops. Palette: whites and royal plum — #EFE9DB flagstone, #6C3FA0 banners, #B99C4A gilt, sky #A9C4D9. Sounds: wind in banners, boot-heel echoes, the great bell of the palace marking hours. Landmarks: **the Coronation Bell** in its open campanile; **the Queen's Old Gate**, a bricked-over archway the guards pretend isn't there (later: the way down to palace_vaults).

Gates: Cinder Row exits to **old_mill**; Ribbon Row's river stairs exit to **river_warehouse**; Larkspur Boulevard's garden gate exits to **silk_grove**; Crownway's Queen's Old Gate exits to **palace_vaults**. **palace_hall** opens off Crownway only in the finale.

## 4. GATHERING ZONES

**old_mill — the Weftworks (tier 1).** An abandoned textile mill on the smoky edge of town, all silent looms, slumped bolts of cloth, and shafts of dusty light through broken skylights. Fabric survives here in forgotten storerooms — bolt-ends of homespun and millspun cotton the creditors never bothered to haul away. Danger: **lintmoths** — plump grey moths that burst in puffs of itchy fluff and chew holes in your gathered cloth (scissor-swipe scatters them), plus **rotten floorboards** that crack underfoot on the upper walkways. Environmental detail: one loom still holds a half-finished baby blanket, threaded and abandoned mid-row; examining it, Marielle quietly finishes three stitches by hand.

**river_warehouse — the Madder Docks (tier 2).** A riverside dye-warehouse district: brick vaults, rocking jetties, and great vats that stain the puddles crimson, indigo, and gold. Imported linens and madder-dyed damasks sit impounded in bonded storage — the excise office sells "spoiled" lots cheap to anyone brave enough to fetch them. Danger: **dye-rats** — rats dyed head-to-tail in vat colors, bold as customs men, who steal loose fabric and must be chased or swiped off. Environmental detail: tally marks on a vat in seven different hands, and one small handprint in indigo, waist-high, decades old.

**silk_grove — the Larkspur Silk Garden (tier 3).** An overgrown silkworm estate behind the Countess's townhouse: mulberry trees swallowed in green, glasshouses gone milky with age, cocoons glinting in the leaves like pale lanterns. Wild grovesilk can be gathered from the untended cocoon nets — with the Countess's blessing, once earned. Danger: **the garden hounds**, two enormous but fundamentally soppy wolfhounds (Pom and Frideric) who bowl trespassers over and confiscate their satchels; move slow, use hedges, or win them over with the sausage Berta packs you. Environmental detail: a child's tea set laid for three inside a glasshouse, one cup holding a perfectly preserved silk rosebud.

**palace_vaults — the Underspool (tier 4).** Forgotten vaults beneath the old palace, reached through the Queen's Old Gate: cold stone, dripping arches, and the royal fabric reserve of a previous century sleeping under dust sheets. Here — and only here — lies **moonweave**, a silver cloth woven under old treaties, faintly luminous in the dark. Danger: **the Warden Loom**, a clockwork loom-sentinel that still patrols on brass rails, sweeping lantern-eyes and snipping shears (hide in alcoves as it passes), plus **crumbling floors** over black cisterns. Environmental detail: a work docket on the wall, signed out and never returned — the last name on it is *B. Thimm*.

## 5. NPC CAST (10 named)

**Odile Marchand** — retired tailor; previous owner of The Gilded Needle, now Marielle's landlady upstairs. Tier: middle. Personality: flinty, kind underneath, arthritis in both hands and opinions in everything else.
- Greeting: *"Rent's due when the month is, and the kettle's on when it isn't. In you come."*
- Hint: *"The Weftworks past Cinder Row — my old suppliers left half their stock to rot there. Mind the floorboards. And the moths. Mostly the moths."*
- Befriended: *"Brida Thimm's granddaughter under my roof. Ha. She'd have laughed her needle loose. She'd also have been proud, girl. So am I."*

**Conductor Cobb** — conductor of the 7:12 mountain line. Tier: poor. Personality: whiskered, punctual to the second, secretly sentimental about every passenger he's ever carried.
- Greeting: *"Ticket! ...Ah, the Hartsfell flag-down. Sit anywhere, miss, the whole carriage is first class if you close one eye."*
- Hint: *"Freight manifest says half of Auberlin's cloth sits impounded down the Madder Docks. Shame, that. Somebody handy ought to do something about it."*
- Befriended: *"I pass Hartsfell twice a week. Say the word and I'll wave at the mountain for you."*

**Berta Klee** — washerwoman of Cinder Row; Marielle's first friend and her "in" with the poor quarter. Tier: poor. Personality: arms like dockrope, laugh like a church bell, mothers everyone within reach.
- Greeting: *"New needle on the square! Let's see your hands. Hm. Honest calluses. You'll do."*
- Hint: *"Millspun from the Weftworks washes up lovely if you can get it past them lintmoths. Take my old beater-paddle— no? Suit yourself, scissors it is."*
- Befriended: *"Winter coat you made Tomas fits like a hug, love. Cinder Row looks after its own, and you're ours now."*

**Sylvie Marsh** — daughter of Marsh & Daughter, Dyes; Marielle's in with Ribbon Row. Tier: middle. Personality: quick, ambitious, talks in colors ("that's a very Tuesday sort of teal").

- Greeting: *"You're the needle from the square! I'm the daughter from the sign. We should be friends immediately."*
- Hint: *"Papa's 'spoiled' lots at the bonded warehouse are barely spoiled — the excise man just can't tell madder from mud. Dye-rats can, mind. Watch your satchel."*
- Befriended: *"When you're famous, tell everyone your reds were Marsh reds. And when I run the company, your thread is free."*

**Countess Elowen Larkspur** — mistress of Larkspur Boulevard's oldest house; Marielle's in with the nobility. Tier: noble. Personality: dry, elegant, lonely; wears forty-year-old gowns because no one since has sewn anything worth wearing.
- Greeting: *"A tailor who walks instead of bowing and scraping her way up the boulevard. How refreshing. Do come in before you develop manners."*
- Hint: *"My grandmother's silk garden still fruits, after a fashion. Mind Pom and Frideric — they have never once caught a burglar but they do try so hard."*
- Befriended: *"This sleeve. My dear girl. I have not worn a new gown since the old queen's day — you may consider that habit broken."*

**Steward Reinholt Quill** — royal steward of the palace; keeper of the coronation. Tier: royal. Personality: immaculate, exhausted, carries a ring of 114 keys and remembers the debt owed to a certain mountain seamstress.
- Greeting: *"You have thirty seconds of my day. ...You are Thimm's granddaughter. You have five minutes of my day."*
- Hint: *"The moonweave sleeps in the Underspool, past the Queen's Old Gate. The Warden Loom still patrols. It was wound in 1861 and no one living knows how to make it stop."*
- Befriended: *"Your grandmother was robbed of her place in the palace ledger. I was a page then, and said nothing. Allow an old man to say something now."*

**Corvin Alba** — of Alba & Sons, Royal Tailors; the rival. Tier: noble. Personality: brilliant scissors, hollow heart; grandson of the man who stole Brida's credit, terrified of being third-rate and certain he is.
- Greeting: *"Ah, the plaza shop. How... rustic. Do keep the moth population down, won't you? For the neighborhood."*
- Hint (a taunt that helps): *"Grovesilk? You? Those hounds would eat a mountain girl whole. Though I suppose dogs do like anything sausage-fed."*
- Befriended (post-rivalry, grudging): *"The gown was better than mine. Both of them were. ...My grandfather kept her sketch, you know. He looked at it every day of his life."*

**Pim** — baker's boy on Spindle Square; flavor NPC. Tier: poor. Personality: nine years old, permanently dusted in flour, self-appointed town crier.
- Greeting: *"EXTRA! New tailor on the square! That's you! You're the extra!"*
- Hint: *"The dye-rats at the docks? I seen a BLUE one. Blue as jam. Well jam's not blue but if it WAS."*
- Befriended: *"Mum says the patch on my elbow's so good I've got to stop climbing things. I'm not going to stop climbing things."*

**Iva the herb-seller** — ancient stall-keeper by the fountain; flavor NPC who knew Brida in the old days. Tier: poor. Personality: sun-dried, sly, speaks mostly in proverbs she is clearly inventing on the spot.
- Greeting: *"Rosemary for remembrance, lavender for moths, and a good morning for free."*
- Hint: *"Old proverb: 'What the palace forgets, the cellar keeps.' Made that one up in 1861, when a mountain girl went down under the Crownway and came up pale."*
- Befriended: *"Brida bought lavender here every market day. You stand just like her, you know. Like a needle: straight, and pointed somewhere."*

**Princess Adelina** — heir to Auberlin, to be crowned at story's end. Tier: royal. Personality: twenty, earnest, secretly dreads the throne; wants to be worth the city she's inheriting.
- Greeting: *"Please don't bow, the crown isn't on yet and my neck aches just thinking of it."*
- Hint: *"They say moonweave shows the wearer's true measure — it dims on liars. You understand my concern with being sewn into it."*
- Befriended: *"Make me a gown I can't lie in, Marielle. If I'm to be queen, let the cloth keep me honest."*

## 6. MAIN QUEST ARC (10 beats)

1. **Stitch One.** Goal: talk to Odile Marchand; sweep out the shop (interact with 3 dusty props); sleep once. Payoff: Odile hands over the keys and Brida's story begins to surface — "Your grandmother stood in this doorway once. Didn't buy anything. Just looked."

2. **Moths in the Weftworks.** Goal: gather 6 Millspun Cotton + 4 Homespun Wool in old_mill; survive the lintmoths. Payoff: first scissor-swipe tutorial; Marielle finishes the abandoned baby blanket, and Berta hears about it by sundown.

3. **Cinder Row's Own.** Goal: craft 3 tier-1 garments (incl. 1 Patched Winter Coat) and sell 3 garments to poor-tier customers; reach reputation 10. Payoff: Berta declares Marielle "ours now"; Cinder Row starts sending customers — and Pim starts shouting about it.

4. **Barely Spoiled.** Goal: talk to Sylvie Marsh; gather 5 Riverflax Linen + 3 Madder Damask in river_warehouse. Payoff: the dye-rats meet their match; Sylvie's father extends shop credit and Ribbon Row opens for business.

5. **The Ribbon Fair Dress.** Goal: craft the Fairday Dress (recipe from Sylvie) and sell 4 garments to middle-tier customers; reach reputation 25. Payoff: Sylvie wears the dress under the Ribbon Arch at the fair; a certain countess's housekeeper takes note. Corvin Alba pays his first sneering visit.

6. **Tea with the Countess.** Goal: talk to Countess Elowen; gather 6 Grovesilk in silk_grove (befriend or evade Pom and Frideric). Payoff: the Countess grants garden rights; in the glasshouse, Marielle finds the silk rosebud — Elowen goes quiet: "My sister made those. Take it. It's been waiting for hands like yours."

7. **The Boulevard Bows.** Goal: craft the Verdigris Evening Gown for Elowen; sell 4 garments to noble-tier customers; reach reputation 50. Payoff: Elowen wears new silk for the first time in decades; Larkspur Boulevard bows. Corvin, rattled, files a complaint that Marielle's shop is "unlicensed for court work" — and Steward Quill comes to investigate personally.

8. **The Name in the Ledger.** Goal: talk to Steward Quill at the palace gate; show him the gilded needle. Payoff: Quill recognizes the needle — and the name. The truth of 1861: Brida Thimm sewed the old queen's coronation gown, and Alba's grandfather took the credit. Quill unlocks the Queen's Old Gate: "The ledger owes your family a line. Go and earn it twice."

9. **The Underspool.** Goal: gather 5 Moonweave in palace_vaults; evade the Warden Loom and the crumbling floors; find Brida's unreturned work docket. Payoff: climax gather. At the docket, a hidden drawer: Brida's original coronation sketch, initialed B.T. — the design Alba's house has imitated for sixty years. Marielle carries both sketch and moonweave into the light.

10. **A Gown That Can't Lie.** Goal: reach reputation 80; craft the Coronation Gown (Moonweave ×5 + Grovesilk ×2 + the gilded needle as tool — an extended stitch-timing sequence, 3 stages); deliver it to Princess Adelina. Payoff: Corvin submits his rival gown, sees Brida's sketch, and withdraws it himself. The coronation is dressed by The Gilded Needle. Proceed to palace_hall.

## 7. ENDING — the Coronation (palace_hall)

Small finale map: banner-hung hall, the Coronation Bell heard through open doors, all befriended NPCs standing in rows — Berta scrubbed pink, Pim on tiptoe, Elowen in verdigris, Corvin in the back, present. Adelina walks the carpet; the moonweave gown glows steady and bright — it does not dim once.

Narration (over the ceremony, one line per slow beat):
1. *"The bell rang, and Auberlin held its breath the way a seamstress holds a thread — gently, and with everything."*
2. *"Queen Adelina walked the long carpet in a gown that could not lie, and it shone the whole way down."*
3. *"In the third row stood a washerwoman, a dye-merchant's daughter, a countess, and a baker's boy who would not stop waving."*
4. *"And in the palace ledger, in fresh ink under an entry sixty years old, the steward wrote two names: Brida Thimm. Marielle Thimm."*
5. *"Grandmother always said a life is sewn one stitch at a time."*
6. *"She never said the last stitch could shine."*

Epilogue card (over the shopfront at dusk, window glowing, bell going *ting*):
*"The Gilded Needle stands on Spindle Square to this day — and every street in Auberlin, from Cinder Row to the Crownway, wears its coats."*

## 8. TONE GUIDE — 5 rules for all in-game text

1. **Warm, never saccharine.** Kindness comes with texture: Odile grumbles while pouring tea, Berta insults you affectionately. If a line could be cross-stitched on a pillow, roughen it.
2. **Class texture without cruelty.** Poverty in Cinder Row is patched and proud, never miserable; nobility is lonely and mannered, never evil. The only villainy is Corvin's insecurity, and even he gets mended, not punished.
3. **Sew the metaphors in sparingly.** One sewing metaphor per scene, maximum, and it must earn its place ("a city stitched out of lamplight"). Never in ordinary shop dialogue — customers talk about weather and rent, not "the fabric of life."
4. **Short lines, storybook cadence.** Narration ≤ 2 lines, dialogue ≤ 2 sentences per box. Prefer concrete nouns (chestnut tree, indigo handprint, 114 keys) over abstractions. Read every line aloud; if you breathe twice, cut it.
5. **Danger is cozy-adventurous.** Threats are described with fond exasperation — moths are "plump," rats are "bold as customs men," the Warden Loom is old machinery doing its job, not a monster. Nothing bleeds, nothing dies; satchels get confiscated, cloth gets nibbled, floors go *crack*.
